about

I created notefeeder to help me keep track of interesting things I found while surfing Reddit and HN so I could find them later. I didn't want to have to leave my current page to do so and wanted the option to jot down any notes or ideas at the same time.

Notefeeder accomplishes this using a simple bookmarklet to create notes in just two clicks and RSS feeds to view what you created.

Its aim is to help you save things you like and what you were thinking at that time so you can come back to them later wherever you are.

notes

Notes are comprised of or one or more of the following parts.
  • title: Just a short(50 character) plain text field. The bookmarklet with auto fill this with the current page's title.
  • url: the link the note is related to.. If the note is small you'll be taken to this url when you click on the note.
  • description: The main body of your note. Supports markdown syntax.

They also have a couple of settings which are:
  • private flag: You can set notes to be private(ish) editing the note and checking the "is private" box. This will still show the notes title but will hide its url and description.
  • completed notes: When you've marked a note as completed it will no longer appear in the lists RSS feed but will still show in the list but will be crossed out. You can unmark a note by editing it and unchecking the "completed" box.

lists

You can use lists to group your notes into logical categories or just use the default list that is always availiable. Each list has an RSS Feed assoicated with it.
